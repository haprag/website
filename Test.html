About Us

The Prag Lab at the University of Manchester, is dedicated to pioneering research in mitochondrial biology and pharmacology. We focus on understanding how mitochondria work in health and disease, including neurodegenerative disorders, cancer and cardiometabolic diseases, and harness this knowledge to develop novel therapeutics combatting these diseases.

Research Highlights

Novel Mitochondrial Drug Targets Identified

Our recent study published in "Nature Metabolism" identifies a groundbreaking new class of mitochondrial targets, offering promising avenues for treating age-related neurodegenerative diseases. This discovery leverages advanced computational modeling and in-vitro validation.

Breakthrough in Cancer Metabolism

We've uncovered a critical metabolic vulnerability in glioblastoma cells, demonstrating how targeting mitochondrial respiration can significantly inhibit tumor growth in preclinical models. This work was featured in "Cell Reports".
